What "MVP" means here

MVP = Minimum Viable Product. Not a prototype. Not a mockup. A real, working product that users can actually use.

The constraint: 1-3 core features max. We build what's essential to validate your idea, nothing more. Here's what fits in 2 weeks:

  • Internal tool: Replace Excel/Google Sheets with a proper web app (CRUD, filtering, exports)
  • Simple marketplace: Connect buyers with sellers (listings, messaging, basic transactions)
  • Booking/scheduling: Calendar-based booking for services in your industry
  • CRM for your niche: Customer management tailored to your specific workflow
  • Dashboard: Visualize internal data with charts and filtering

What doesn't fit: Social networks, "Uber for X", AI/ML-heavy apps, games. These need months, not weeks.

Common questions

Is 2 weeks really enough for an MVP?
Yes, for a properly scoped MVP with 1-3 core features. The key is focus: we build what's essential to validate your idea, nothing more. If you have 10 features in mind, we'll discuss which 3 are truly critical and build those first. You can always add more features later once you've validated the core concept.
What happens after the 2 weeks?
You own 100% of the code and can do whatever you want with it. Hire another developer, bring it in-house, or continue working with me on an ongoing basis (€100/hour or fixed-price projects for additional features). Most founders choose to validate first, then expand if the MVP shows traction.
Do I need to be technical?
No. I've worked with many non-technical founders (ex-consultants, PMs, marketers). You just need to clearly explain the problem you're solving and who your users are. I'll handle all the technical decisions and explain things in plain English. We'll have daily check-ins so you're never in the dark.
Can you build my exact idea for [competitor]?
Probably not in 2 weeks. Apps like Instagram, Uber, Airbnb took teams of engineers months or years. But we can build a simplified version focused on your unique angle. For example: not "Uber for X" (too complex), but "simple booking system for X industry" (totally doable). The scope conversation on our discovery call will clarify what fits.
What tech stack do you use?
Default: React/Next.js frontend, Node.js or Python backend, PostgreSQL database via Supabase, deployed to Vercel or Railway. But I adapt based on your needs. Already have a team using a specific stack? I can match it. Need something specific for your industry? Let's discuss.
What if I'm not sure about the scope?
That's what the discovery call is for. We'll spend 30-45 minutes discussing your idea, your users, and what success looks like. I'll ask clarifying questions and help you identify the 1-3 features that are truly essential. By the end, you'll have a clear scope and fixed price quote.
